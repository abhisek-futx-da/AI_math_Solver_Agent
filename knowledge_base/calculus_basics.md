# Basic Calculus - Limits and Derivatives

## Limits
- lim_{x→a} f(x) = L means f(x) gets arbitrarily close to L as x → a
- lim_{x→0} sin(x)/x = 1; lim_{x→0} (1+x)^(1/x) = e
- Limit exists iff left and right limits exist and are equal

## Derivatives
- f'(x) = lim_{h→0} (f(x+h) - f(x))/h
- (x^n)' = n x^(n-1); (e^x)' = e^x; (ln x)' = 1/x (x>0)
- (sin x)' = cos x; (cos x)' = -sin x
- Product: (uv)' = u'v + uv'; Quotient: (u/v)' = (u'v - uv')/v²
- Chain: (f(g(x)))' = f'(g(x)) g'(x)

## Optimization
- Critical points: solve f'(x) = 0 (and where f' undefined)
- Second derivative test: f''(x)>0 ⇒ local min; f''(x)<0 ⇒ local max
- Domain constraints: always check boundary and domain (e.g. x > 0)
